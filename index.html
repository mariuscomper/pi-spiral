π Spiral

768 digits of π mapped onto an Archimedean spiral, each coloured by its value — from the first decimal outward to the Feynman Point and six digits beyond.

Hover or tap to explore

The Feynman Point — six consecutive 9s beginning at the 762nd decimal place — is ringed in gold. Richard Feynman once joked he'd like to recite π up to that point and say "nine nine nine nine nine nine and so on", implying a false pattern.
